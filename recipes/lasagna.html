Lasagna recipe

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made by layering wide, flat pasta sheets with rich meat or vegetable sauce, creamy ricotta cheese, mozzarella, and Parmesan, then baking it until bubbly and golden. The dish is known for its hearty, comforting flavors and satisfying layers, making it a favorite in many households. Traditionally, lasagna is made with a tomato-based meat sauce, but variations include white sauce (béchamel), vegetarian versions with spinach and mushrooms, or even seafood-based recipes.

Originating from Italy, specifically from regions like Emilia-Romagna, lasagna has evolved into different styles worldwide. The traditional Lasagna alla Bolognese uses a slow-cooked ragù (meat sauce) and béchamel instead of ricotta, while American-style lasagna typically includes ricotta and more mozzarella for a cheesier texture. Whether homemade or store-bought, lasagna is a beloved dish that brings people together, perfect for family dinners, holidays, or special occasions.

Ingredients

For the meat sauce

  • 1 lb (450g) ground beef or Italian sausage
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 (28 oz) can crushed tomatoes
  • (15 oz) can tomato sauce
  • 1 (6 oz) can tomato paste
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • ½ tsp red pepper flakes (optional)
  • 1 tsp sugar (to balance acidity)

For the Ricotta Mixture

  • 15 oz (425g) ricotta cheese
  • 1 egg
  • ¼ cup chopped fresh parsley (or 1 tbsp dried)
  • ½ tsp salt

Other Ingredients

  • 12 lasagna noodles (regular or oven-ready)
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
